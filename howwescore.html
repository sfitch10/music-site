Methodology

How We Score

No black boxes. Here's exactly how a number becomes a score.

Our Two Scores

Every album on this site receives two independent scores: The Stage and The Crowd. These are combined into a single number — the Combined Score — that you see on every album card and page.

We keep them separate because they measure different things. The Stage measures critical consensus. The Crowd measures real listening behavior. Neither one alone tells the full story.

🎙️ The Stage

The Stage score comes from professional music critics at established outlets. We pull scores from:

Pitchfork Rolling Stone NME AllMusic The Guardian Paste Magazine Consequence of Sound

Each outlet has reviewed thousands of albums and has established critical credibility over years of publication. We normalize their different scoring scales — stars, letter grades, decimals — into a single comparable number so they can be weighted and combined.

Not every outlet reviews every album. When an outlet hasn't reviewed an album, we redistribute its weight proportionally across outlets that did. Albums with fewer than three outlet reviews receive a Limited Data flag, indicating the score should be treated with caution.

👥 The Crowd

The Crowd score reflects how people are actually engaging with an album — through streaming data, chart performance, and the vibe taps you leave on this site.

Unlike sites that rely on self-reported user ratings (which are easy to manipulate and don't reflect actual listening), we anchor the crowd score in real listening behavior. Streaming numbers don't lie. Chart weeks don't lie. The vibe taps are a smaller but genuinely human signal layered on top.

Streaming data is normalized for time — an album that was released five years ago has had five years to accumulate plays, while something that dropped last month has had 30 days. We account for that so newer albums aren't automatically at a disadvantage.

The Combined Score

We blend The Stage and The Crowd into a single score. The exact blend depends on which ranking you're looking at:

Monthly Rankings

The Crowd carries slightly more weight. Right now matters — what people are actually listening to today is the signal.

All-Time Rankings

Critics carry slightly more weight. History has had more time to judge. Critical consensus tends to age better than streaming velocity.

Score Tiers

Every Combined Score maps to a tier badge. Tiers are determined by score ranges — there are no arbitrary editorial adjustments. If an album scores in a range, it gets that badge, full stop.

🔥 Certified Banger The top of the pile. Few albums get here.
🤘 Absolute Slapper Excellent. Strong critical and crowd reception.
🎧 Hard Rotation Good. Worth your time.
😐 Mid Season Fine. Mixed signals from both sides.
💀 Deep Cut For completionists only.

Gap Badges

Sometimes The Stage and The Crowd disagree significantly. When they do, we surface that disagreement with a Gap Badge — an honest acknowledgment that something interesting is happening.

👑 People's Champion The crowd loves it. Critics are cold.
💎 Critics' Darling Critics love it. The crowd is lukewarm.
🤝 Unanimous Rare. Both sides agree within 5 points.

On Deck

New albums enter a 30-day evaluation window called On Deck. During this window, the score is still forming — critic reviews are coming in, streaming data is accumulating, chart positions are being established.

We show On Deck albums separately from the main catalog with a progress bar indicating how many days remain. After 30 days, the score locks and the album graduates to the main rankings.

On Deck scores are real — you can see the early signal forming in real time. We just don't want you to mistake an early score for a settled one.

What We Don't Do

We don't let labels or artists pay for placement. Score is the only thing that determines ranking position.

We don't adjust scores based on an album's popularity, controversy, or the artist's cultural moment. The math is the math.

We don't remove albums once they're scored. If an album drops in relevance over time, that'll show up in the streaming data — but we don't scrub history.

We don't have user accounts or verified critics. The vibe tap is anonymous and lightweight by design.

The score is the score. We built a system we trust, and we let it run.
